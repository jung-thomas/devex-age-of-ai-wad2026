title: The DevEx gap in the AI era
---

---
layout: title-text
title: Generic AI isn't enough
---

Enterprise developers don't need *another* chat window. They need
intelligence that understands their context: the codebase, the
data model, the deployment target, the compliance constraints.

<!--
Speaker notes: lead with the friction story — every dev advocate
has heard "ChatGPT is great but it doesn't know our system."

---
layout: divider
title: Embedding intelligence in the workflow
---

---
layout: title-text
title: Where developers already are
---

The win isn't a better assistant — it's an assistant that shows
up at the right moment inside the IDE, the CLI, the docs, the
pipeline. **Right tool, right context, right time.**

---
layout: divider
title: Rebuilding learning with generative AI
---

---
layout: title-text
title: Tutorials that adapt to you
---

Static "Hello World" tutorials don't scale across thousands of
products and personas. Generative AI lets us produce learning
paths that respond to what each developer is actually building.

---
layout: divider
title: Listening at scale
---

---
layout: title-text
title: Feedback as a first-class signal
---

When 100,000+ developers use your platform, surveys aren't the
fastest signal. Instrumented IDE telemetry + community sentiment
analysis surface issues before the support backlog does.

---
layout: divider
title: Education + community = adoption
---

---
layout: title-text
title: Humans + AI, not humans or AI
---

Automated guidance gets developers unstuck for the first 80%.
Community — peers, advocates, champions — handles the long tail.
Neither alone scales; together they cover the gap.
